Frequently Asked Questions

A Schneider Arc Fault Detection Device (AFDD) continuously monitors electrical circuits and detects dangerous arc faults caused by damaged cables, loose connections or insulation failures. When an arc fault is detected, the AFDD disconnects the circuit, helping prevent electrical fires and improving overall safety.

An MCB protects against overloads and short circuits, an RCCB protects against earth leakage and electric shock, while an AFDD detects dangerous arc faults that may cause electrical fires. AFDDs provide an additional layer of protection beyond conventional circuit protection devices.

Schneider AFDD solutions are commonly installed in residential buildings, hospitals, schools, hotels, offices, commercial facilities and public infrastructure where fire prevention and electrical safety are critical requirements.

AFDDs help detect hazardous electrical conditions that may not be identified by traditional protection devices. By detecting arc faults early, they help reduce fire risks, protect property and improve compliance with modern electrical safety standards.

Schneider Electric Acti9 Active AFDD solutions combine advanced arc fault detection technology, reliable performance and enhanced electrical safety. They are designed to help protect people, property and critical installations from electrical fire hazards.
