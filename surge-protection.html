Frequently Asked Questions

A Schneider Surge Protection Device (SPD) protects electrical systems and connected equipment from transient overvoltages caused by lightning strikes, switching operations and power surges. SPDs help prevent equipment damage, downtime and costly electrical failures.

Type 1 SPDs protect against direct lightning currents, Type 2 SPDs protect against switching surges and indirect lightning effects, while Type 3 SPDs provide additional protection for sensitive electronic equipment located close to the load.

Schneider Electric offers Acti9 iPRD, Acti9 iQuick PRD and Easy9 SPD solutions. The right selection depends on the installation type, surge risk level, discharge capacity requirements and protection objectives.

Schneider SPDs are commonly installed in main distribution boards, sub-distribution panels, industrial control panels, data centers, hospitals and commercial buildings to provide comprehensive surge protection throughout the electrical system.

Schneider Acti9 iPRD and Easy9 SPD devices offer reliable lightning protection, high surge discharge capacity, compliance with international standards and long-term protection for critical electrical and electronic equipment.
